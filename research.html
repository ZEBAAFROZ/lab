Our Research

At FLaME Research Lab, our work is driven by the belief that language technology can transform the way we interact with the world. We span multiple high-impact domains of NLP.

Our research covers Mental Health, Fake News, Claims, Propaganda, and Deception where we apply cutting-edge NLP techniques to address complex challenges. Our projects push the boundaries of theoretical understanding while offering practical solutions to real-world problems.

In each project, we strive to combine rigorous research methodologies with innovative approaches, ensuring that our contributions are both impactful and relevant. We take pride in sharing our findings with the broader NLP and AI community.

Automatic Speech Recognition

The goal is to improve the existing state-of-the-art ASR systems or propose a new model for automatic speech recognition for code-mixed languages. We work on cross-lingual transfer learning, data augmentation, and neural architectures specifically designed to handle the phonological and lexical challenges of languages like Hindi-English (Hinglish). Our work addresses a critical gap in speech technology for South Asian multilingual speakers.

Healthcare & Mental Health AI

We develop AI-driven models for healthcare — specifically for mental health counseling and learning disorders. Our systems analyze peer support conversations in online mental health communities, identify behavioral traits that foster engagement, and generate supportive responses. Projects like the EMNLP 2024 counseling summarization work demonstrate how LLMs can be guided by domain knowledge to produce clinically relevant outputs.

Explainable AI

The goal is to use knowledge graphs and LLMs for explaining social biases, hate speech, sarcasm, and other complex linguistic phenomena. Rather than treating NLP models as black boxes, we build systems that produce human-interpretable explanations alongside their predictions. Our work on HateXplain and sarcasm explanation generation are key contributions in this space.

Information Disorder

This project is dedicated to developing advanced models for identifying and mitigating harmful content on social media platforms. We focus on combating the spread of fake news, misinformation, propaganda, and hate speech. Our approaches include multimodal learning (text + image), commonsense knowledge injection, and cross-lingual transfer to build robust detectors that work across languages and platforms.

COIL-D (in collaboration with Bhasini)

The project COIL-D is in collaboration with Bhasini which aims at: promoting the creation of language resources for Human Language Technology (HLT) applications, especially for Machine Translation; creating leaderboards for Machine Translation evaluation; and establishing benchmark setups for various linguistic tools. This work directly supports the Bhasini initiative to advance language technology for Indian languages.
